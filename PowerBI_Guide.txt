Before you start:
  - Make sure you have already run clean_data.py so the file
    data/cleaned_stock_data.csv exists in your project folder.
  - Download and install Power BI Desktop (FREE) from:
    https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/download/details.aspx?id=58494

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
PART 1 — LOAD YOUR DATA INTO POWER BI
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Step 1:  Open Power BI Desktop.
         You will see a splash screen. Click "Get data" OR close the splash
         and click "Get data" from the Home ribbon at the top.

Step 2:  In the "Get data" window, type "Text/CSV" in the search box.
         Click "Text/CSV" and click "Connect".

Step 3:  A file browser opens. Navigate to your project folder:
           D:\Priyanka\AIML_Data Driven Stock Analysis MP 2\stock_analysis\data\
         Select "cleaned_stock_data.csv" and click "Open".

Step 4:  A preview window shows your data. You will see columns like:
         ticker, date, open, close, high, low, volume, daily_return,
         Sector, month_name, etc.
         Click "Load" (bottom right).

Step 5:  Wait a few seconds. Power BI loads ~50,000 rows.
         On the right side you will now see a "Fields" panel listing all columns.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
PART 2 — ADD COMPUTED COLUMNS (Calculated Measures)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

You need to create two "Measures" — these are formulas Power BI uses to
calculate values dynamically.

Step 6:  In the Home ribbon, click "New Measure" (or right-click your table
         name in the Fields panel and choose "New Measure").

Step 7:  The formula bar appears at the top. Delete what's there and type
         this formula exactly:

           Avg Close Price = AVERAGE(cleaned_stock_data[close])

         Press Enter. This measure will appear in your Fields panel.

Step 8:  Create another New Measure. Type:

           Avg Daily Volume = AVERAGE(cleaned_stock_data[volume])

         Press Enter.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
PART 3 — BUILD THE VISUALS (Charts)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

You will build 6 visuals on one page. Click on an empty area of the canvas
before starting each visual.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VISUAL 1 — Card: Average Close Price
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Step 9:  In the Visualizations panel (right side), click the "Card" icon.
         (It looks like a rectangle with "123" inside it.)

Step 10: In the Fields panel on the right, find your measure "Avg Close Price".
         Drag it into the "Fields" box of the Card visual (shown in the
         Visualizations panel under "Build visual").

Step 11: The card will now display the average close price.
         Click somewhere else to deselect, then repeat for next visual.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VISUAL 2 — Card: Average Daily Volume
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Step 12: Click on empty canvas area.
         Click the "Card" icon again.
         Drag "Avg Daily Volume" measure into the Fields box.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VISUAL 3 — Bar Chart: Top 10 Gainers & Losers by Yearly Return
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Step 13: Click on empty canvas area.
         In the Visualizations panel, click the "Clustered Bar Chart" icon
         (horizontal bars).

Step 14: From the Fields panel, drag:
           "ticker"       → into the "Y-axis" box
           "close"        → into the "X-axis" box
           (Power BI will auto-sum it; we just need a reference visual here)

Step 15: With this visual selected, click the "Filters" pane (top right,
         funnel icon). Under "Filters on this visual", find "ticker".
         Set it to show only the top 10 tickers you know are gainers
         (TRENT, BHARTIARTL, etc.) — or skip this filter for now.

         Note: Power BI does not auto-rank like Python. This visual gives
         a general performance overview. You will refine it after seeing
         the data.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VISUAL 4 — Line Chart: Stock Price Over Time (for top stocks)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Step 16: Click on empty canvas area.
         In the Visualizations panel, click "Line Chart".

Step 17: Drag these fields:
           "date"   → X-axis
           "close"  → Y-axis
           "ticker" → Legend

Step 18: With the visual selected, go to the Filters pane.
         Under "Filters on this visual", find "ticker".
         Change filter type to "Top N".
         Set: Show Top 5, By value: close (Sum).
         Click "Apply filter".
         Now the chart shows only the 5 highest-priced stocks over time.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VISUAL 5 — Bar Chart: Average Yearly Return by Sector
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Step 19: Click on empty canvas area.
         Click "Clustered Column Chart" (vertical bars).

Step 20: Drag:
           "Sector"       → X-axis
           "daily_return" → Y-axis  (Power BI will show average automatically)

Step 21: With the visual selected, go to the Format pane (paint roller icon).
         Click "Columns" → "Colors".
         You can set a conditional color (green for positive, red for negative)
         later. For now, the chart correctly shows which sectors performed best.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VISUAL 6 — Slicer: Filter by Sector (Interactive Filter)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Step 22: Click on empty canvas area.
         In the Visualizations panel, click "Slicer" (the funnel icon).

Step 23: Drag "Sector" from the Fields panel into the "Field" box.
         A dropdown slicer appears. Now when you click a sector in this
         slicer, ALL other visuals on the page update automatically.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
PART 4 — FORMAT & ARRANGE
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Step 24: Arrange your visuals on the canvas:
         - Put the two Cards (Avg Price, Avg Volume) at the top
         - Put the Slicer top-right
         - Bar charts in the middle
         - Line chart at the bottom (it's wider)

Step 25: To add a title to the page:
         Click "Insert" in the top ribbon → "Text Box".
         Type: "Nifty 50 Stock Performance Dashboard"
         Make it large (font size 20+) and place it at the very top.

Step 26: To give each visual a title:
         Click a visual → Format pane (paint roller) → "Title" → toggle On.
         Type a meaningful title like "Average Return by Sector".

Step 27: Change the page theme (optional but looks professional):
         View ribbon → Themes → pick any theme you like.

PART 5 — SAVE & EXPORT
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Step 28: Save your Power BI file:
         File → Save As → navigate to:
           D:\Priyanka\AIML_Data Driven Stock Analysis MP 2\stock_analysis\
         Save as: "Nifty50_Dashboard.pbix"

Step 29: Export as PDF for submission:
         File → Export → Export to PDF.
         Save it in the same folder as "Nifty50_Dashboard.pdf".
